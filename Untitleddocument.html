Kickstarter is essentially an online market that has both creator and supporter. For an online market to grow, one of the most important feature that cannot be ignored is the "Network effect". This means that Kickstarter needs to grow both its number of creators and number of supporters. A creator is someone or some company that has an idea that needs funding to let it come to fruition. A supporter is an online user on the Kickstarter who decides to contribute his or her money for the project created by the creator. Because Kickstarter makes money when a project is successfully funded (5% of the money funded goes to the platform according to Investopedia), it is very important for Kickstarter to make sure a creator's project meets the funding goal. Therefore, the aim of this project is to help both creator and the platform to increase success rate and therefore revenue.

(Fig.1)

United States makes up 77% percentage of the project listings on Kickstarter (Fig.1). It is important to analyze listings in the US first to see what Kickstarter can do to improve success rate and thus generate more revenue.

(Fig.2)

There are only four categories which have a successful funding rate above 50% (number of funded projects/total number of projects). The highlighted dot (Fig.2) represents Music category, which has the second most number of project listed and highest amount of projects funded, and of course, a successful rate above 50%. Film and Video category has the most projects, but does not have as many funded projects as the Music category. In fact, Music category has the fourth highest (Fig.3) successful rate out of 15 major category, whereas Film and Video category has an almost average successful rate of 38% (the horizontal line indicates average successful rate).

(Fig.3)

Both Music and Film and Video represent a tremendous revenue making opportunity for Kickstarter. Film and Video has 51,992 projects listed, and Music has 43,238 projects listed. This represents 95,230 projects listed (Fig.4), about 32.5% of all the US listings, and 25% of all the listings on Kickstarter. Two things that can generate more revenue for Kickstarter : 1) increase the success rate for Film and Video category 2) increase the listings for Music category.

(Fig.4)

How much revenue can improved success rate in Film and Video and increase listings in Music generate for Kickstarter? For Film and Video, the median amount funded for all the successful listings are $5,000 (Fig.5). If the successful rate can be improved from its current 38% to 50%, which means around 6959 more successful projects, there will be $34,795,000 extra funding, $1,739,900 in terms of revenue, just from this category.

 

(Fig.5)

For Music category (Fig.6), if the project listings can be increased to as many as Film and Video, about 8754 more listings, an extra funding around $30,639,000, $1,531,950 extra revenue, will be generated given the median funding for successful projects in this category.

(Fig.6)

All together, by increasing the number of listings in Music category and by increasing the success rate in Film and Video category, Kickstarter will be able to generate an extra $3,327,850 revenue from mid 2011 to early 2018. That is about $503,361 per year lost in revenue for Kickstarter.

(Fig.7)

Focusing on improving these two categories is not enough, however. Technology category has the lowest success rate, 22% (Fig.3), and yet has an average 796 backers, the second highest, for each project under its category. This represents a tremendous waste of supporter’s time and money and also creator’s effort putting in listing the project on Kickstarter. On average, a successful project only asks for 270 backers (Fig.8), not 796 backers.

(Fig.8)

 In short, many supporters funded projects that will fail, and many creators create projects that will not be funded. Kickstarter not only lost revenue from technology listing, but also gives an idea, perhaps, to first time supporters that projects on Kickstarter are meant to fail. Instead, Kickstarter should look into why these projects fail and why these projects attract supporters in the first place. One reason, for example, could be the amount of funding demanded for supporters (Fig.9). The median funding asked is $10,800, which is twice as much as Film and Video asked. Kickstarter should alert creators in technology space to lower their scope of project, and therefore funding, in order to achieve more success. However, this is not the whole story. Projects in technology receives attention because of what the projects promise.One place to look is “Purple® Pillow: The World's First No-Pressure Pillow.”This project in technology has been overfunded for more than 10000%, that is 100 times the original goal. The project has a very detailed description and various images of the products. Supporters who funded this project will likely to come back and recommend this website to others because they are finding a project that interests them and a project that can potentially improve their sleeping quality. Therefore, Kickstarter should also encourage creators to create projects with detailed descriptions and images.

How much potential revenue is represent in this space? On average, the projects that have been successfully funded have a median funding goal of $4,000(Fig.10). Assuming now all the technology projects have adjusted their scope and funding demand to $4,000, and improved their success rate from  22% to average, which is 38%, an extra $13.7 million funding, $685,000 revenue, could be generated. Furthermore, the resources that has been given to projects in technology will no longer be wasted, and less funding asked means more money will go to other projects and category, perhaps Music and Film and Video.

From this brief discussion above, for listings in the US, Kickstarter could have generated almost $4 million extra revenue from three categories discussed above. Therefore, Kickstarter should aim to improve the success rate for Film and Video, increase listings in Music, and educate creators in technology category to generate more revenue in the years to come.
